\textbf{Question:} \\ \\The $4^{th}$ term of a G.P. is square of its second term, and the first term is -3. Determine its $7^{th}$ term.\\

\textbf{Solution:} \\ \\
Let, first term of this G.P.(X(0)) be a.
\\Given, the first term is -3.\\ i.e. a= -3 (given).......(1) \\ 
Let r be the common ratio of G.P.\\
Given that the fourth term of G.P. is square of its second term.\\
\\We know $n^{th}$ term of a G.P. can be written as : $ar^{n-1}$......(2)
\begin{align}
 X(3)=(X(1))^2\hspace{0.5cm}(Given)......(3) 
 \end{align}
 \hspace{7cm} substituting (2) in (3),
 \begin{align}
 ar^{4-1}=(ar^{2-1})^2\\
ar^3=a^2r^2\\
r=a\\
(from(1))\hspace{0.4cm}r=-3.......(4)\\
7^{th} term(X(6))=ar^{7-1}
\end{align}
\hspace{7cm} from (1) and (4)
\begin{align}
X(6)=(-3)(-3)^6\\
X(6)=(-3)^7=-2187
\end{align}
\begin{table}[h]
  \centering
  \begin{tabular}{|c|c|}
    \hline
    \textbf{Variable} & \textbf{Description} \\
    \hline
    a & first term of G.P. \\
    \hline
    r & Common ratio of G.P. \\
    \hline
    X(n-1) & nth term of the G.P. \\
    \hline
  \end{tabular}
  \caption{A Table with input parameters}
  \label{tab:1}
\end{table}
So $7^{th}$ term of the G.P. is -2187.
